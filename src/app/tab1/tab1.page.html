Big Tree Bonsai

It's an old hobby

Bonsai cultivation is almost nearly 2000 years, with bonsai being spotted in records dating back to the earliest dynasties.
Back then they were just small trees placed in containers: it was the Japanese that took the current interpretation of the bonsai and developed it into the hobby we know of today.

China vs Japan

Chinese and Japanese bonsai are very different. Perhaps owing to the difference in cultures.
Japanse bonsai are more rigid, and are tightly pruned to maintain the shape of the tree, whereas Chinese bonsai growers typically allow for a more freeform, unorthodox shape.

Patience is a virture

One of the most important aspects of bonsai cultivation is embracing the learning curve and leaving room for trial and error.
Every tree has its own share of faults, so it's vital that bonsai growers do their fair share of research to see how they want their trees to turn out after the growing process

Formal and Informal bonsai

There are two main types Bonsai styles, Formal and Informal
The classic, 'formal' style (Koten) has a wider trunk at the base which tapers off towards the top
The informal, or 'Comic' style (Bunjin) is the complete opposite , with a top end much wider than the rest of the trunk
